Work

I have a music degree and began my working life as a secondary school music teacher. After a brief stint as acting head of department, I took a break to do an MSc in Music Technology at York University with a view to teaching Music Technology at A Level.

Instead, I fell in love with coding and never went back. In the past 12 years I've worked at a mixture of small companies and large, initially as a Java developer but with more emphasis on Python and UI work in recent years.

(However, I haven't completely escaped - my Code Club at Tang Hall Primary is now in its second year).

Stashmetrics Jan 2016

I'm very happy to have been asked to come back to Stashmetrics, this time as a permanent employee. I work across the full stack, including Angular, mongoDB, AWS, Java and Apache Storm.

Yossarian Mar 2015

Yossarian are building a search engine with a difference. Traditional search engines can end up telling us what we already know, but Yossarian aim to return results that are disparate, but related (my personal favourite: heaven // tea).

While I was there I worked on their infrastructure, including AWS, Chef, Titan and Cassandra and Hadoop as well as the usual Java and front end work.

Stashmetrics Jan 2014

In January 2014, I was the first person hired by Stashmetrics, a social media analytics company. When I arrived, Mike and Sam had just received funding from Collider and were busy building a product really really fast to start selling to clients as soon as possible.

In the three months I was there, I pulled together a coherent Java backend from Mike's prototypes and wrote the first dashboards. This was my first commercial experience of Meteor and the initial feedback from clients was really positive. I left in April 2014 to have my second child, but continued to do some freelance work with them over the next year.

Panoplia Jun 2013

Panoplia are a startup specialising in security solutions. They began as a consultancy business but now have several software projects at the prototyping stage that they aim to take to market. They recruited me early to be their first employee and I was responsible for taking their flagship product, Doqex, from prototype to launch.

CPP Aug 2010

From July 2012 I worked on a project to migrate all CPP's customer-facing websites from WebLogic to JBoss. Before this I developed a website used by call centre staff to answer queries on the various insurance products CPP provide. The main technologies were HTML, CSS, JavaScript, jQuery, JSF and Java.

WiMAC Jan 2010

WiMAC are a small company who produce embedded and JEE software for controlling street lighting. My role was to oversee the Java development of their online configuration tools.

They had previously had no Java team lead, so I became responsible for setting up much of the infrastructure for ensuring best practice, including unit testing, continuous integration using Hudson (now Jenkins), bug tracking with Bugzilla and a wiki using MoinMoin. I also handled much of the systems administration, setting up and configuring MySQL, Tomcat and Apache on their RedHat servers.

ioko (now Piksel) Mar 2005

At ioko I worked on what is now Sky Go. The November 2009 release included support for viewing content on the Xbox games console and I was the main developer working on the SOAP Java API.

Before that I worked for a client in the pharmaceutical sector, developing and maintaining a system for secure data collection, manipulation and delivery from thousands of pharmacy shops into the client's systems. I also helped develop an ISP service for these pharmacies, including access to the NHS N3 network and remote configuration and update of routers at individual pharmacies.
