--- title: "Days 13-18: Quora Insincere Questions Classifier" created: !!timestamp '2019-01-31 12:00:00' --- {% mark excerpt -%} Following my relative success with the [spam classifier](spam-classifier.html), I decided that a good next step would be to enter a competition on [Kaggle](https://www.kaggle.com/), for cash, to [classify questions on Quora](https://www.kaggle.com/c/quora-insincere-questions-classification). I mean, I've worked alongside data scientists 3 times now (and even understood a lot of what they said), I've taken a [course](https://www.coursera.org/learn/machine-learning/), and I've built a working [spam classifier](https://github.com/hmcc/spam-classifier). How hard could it be? It turns out it's actually quite hard. {%- endmark %} {% mark image -%} ![Top of the Kaggle leaderboard with an accuracy of 0.72)]([[!!img/kaggle-leaderboard-top.png]]) {%- endmark %} {% mark image -%} ![Bottom of the Kaggle leaderboard with my accuracy of 0.587)]([[!!img/kaggle-leaderboard-me.png]]) {%- endmark %} So what went wrong? * The task is as much an NLP task as a ML one. I've done a little NLP, but it was far too [specific](https://www.cl.cam.ac.uk/~es407/papers/Coling10.pdf) to be any use here. I'm going to add [this introductory NLP course](http://web.stanford.edu/class/cs224n/) to my todo list! * I wasn't social enough - many of the top performing kernels were based on the work of others. * My spam classifier didn't scale well. I was aware that it wouldn't, but I underestimated how much time it would take me to fix this, meaning that I didn't really have enough time overall. I would have loved to keep going with it, because it's been a lot of fun, but at 4 weeks into a 6 week "[retreat](retreat.html)" I think I need to be a bit more selective about how I spend my time, and a lot less ambitious :)
